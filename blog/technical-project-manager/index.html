About us

Resolve to Save Lives is an initiative of Vital Strategies with a mission to prevent 100 million deaths from heart disease and to make the world safer from epidemics. We are led by Dr. Tom Frieden, former director of the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Our focus is maximizing our global impact with speed, simplicity, and scale.

Read more about our work at resolvetosavelives.org.

About our work

Our team develops an ultra-lightweight data recording and visualization tool called Simple that is used by thousands of healthcare workers in India, Bangladesh, and Ethiopia to manage 800,000+ patients with hypertension and diabetes. We're also piloting software for hypertension control in Nigeria.

Simple is software that saves lives. Our Android app is used by nurses and healthcare workers in thousands of clinics every day, recording basic patient details, blood pressures, and medications. It enables doctors to track patient treatment over time and remind patients to return for care, both critical elements for controlling high blood pressures. Our app is designed offline-first, allowing it to function quickly and reliably in environments with limited network access.

We also provide a web dashboard to healthcare workers and public health experts, enabling them to see where and how people are getting healthier. This dashboard is vital to helping improve blood pressure control rates across large populations, but also includes detailed information to help improve individual clinics.

Learn more about the Simple project at Simple.org and in this article.

About the role

We're looking for qualified candidates for the position of Technical Project Manager. This person will help to coordinate the development and deployment of flexible and powerful software for public health.

This role will require project management skills to support software development, rollout and constant improvement and close partner management with key stakeholders including, the World Health Organization, government partners, and field staff who work in health facilities. This is our first formal project manager on this project, so you will be formalizing informal processes, bringing rigor to scheduling and ticketing, and taking significant ownership over processes. We are looking for someone who is excited to bring their skills to a project that has the potential to save many lives and requires constant learning and adaptation as we grow.

This is a full-time role reporting to our Director of Engineering.
